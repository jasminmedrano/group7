    Endangered Species & Ecosystems is an interactable site where you can explore different ecosystems and learn about a few endangered species that live within those biomes. These species can range from conversation status of vulnerable to nearly extinct.

    Jaguar

    “I am a Jaguar in the large cat species. I can be up to 6 ft and one inch.
    I am the third largest cat in the world. I am pale yellow to tan colored fur with spots.
    I have a very powerful bite; I can break through a turtle's shell. But my habitat is in DANGER!
    Due to fragmentation meaning big parts of my habitat are destroyed that it is hard to regrow over time!
    long with human poaching who wants my fur! There's only 15,000 jaguars left in the world!
    If I go extinct the food chain will be disrupted and the rodent population will rise!”

    Giant Otter

    I am a Giant Otter. I live in the Amazon River and Pantanal.
    I am the largest member of the weasel family…
    Or atleast will be if humans would stop poaching my speeches for my pelt.
    There are only 5000 left of my species!
    Without my species I can't control the prey species population in the river to maintain a healthy ecosystem.

    South American Tapir

    I am a South American Tapir.
    I am one of the largest surviving native mammals in the Amazon.
    I am dark brown with a paler skin around my face.
    I can live up to 25-30 years! I am an excellent swimmer and can move quicker on land…
    But not fast enough to run away from poachers that want my meat and hide.
    There are only 4,000 of my species left!
    Without my species the ecosystem would not provide the same rich variety of animals and plants.
    We are known as the umbrella species because we have many habitats that include different species throughout.

    Hyacinth Macaws

    I am Hyacinth Macaw. I am native to Central and Eastern South America.
    I am the largest macaw and flying parrot species in the world.
    My tail is long and pointed. My feathers are entirely blue and my neck can be a different color.
    I am losing my habitat due to humans trapping wild birds for pet trade!
    Without my species we can't germinate or grow vegetation to grow when we eat seeds.
    There are only 6,500 left of my species!

    Tropical

    Deforestation is the leading cause of endangered species in tropical and jungle ecosystems.

    Saharan Cheetah

    I am a Saharan Cheetah! I am mostly nocturnal.
    I am most active between sunset and early mornings.
    I have a short coat and almost white in color with spots. I live in Western and Central Sahara.
    I can travel large distances but I try not to.
    Unfortunately, due to some prey that we feed on are going extinct we tend to starve to death.
    Along with our cubs who often get eaten by other predators.
    One of the biggest threats to us are humans that poach us for illegal swaps of our body parts and fur.
    There are only 250 Saharan Cheetahs left!

    North African Ostrich

    I am a North African Ostrich! I am the largest living bird!
    I live from Western to Northeastern Africa.
    Luckily, lately I been getting a lot of help from many conservation projects.
    I can travel large distances but I try not to.
    There is only 100 of us left in the world.
    Due to humans hunting us for our feathers, eggs, and food.

    Egyptian Tortoise

    I am a Egyptian Tortoise! I am native to Egypt, Libya, and Isreal!
    I am the smallest tortoise in the Northern Hemisphere.
    I can range in colors from pale, brown to pink or yellow.
    My colors helps regulate the impact of sunlight.
    There is only 7,000 of us left in the world.
    Due to habitat loss and humans collecting us for pet trade.

    Peninsular Bighorn Sheep

    I am a Peninsular Bighorn Sheep! I can go without water for extended periods of time.
    I am the state mammal of Nevada.
    I am heavy and can weigh up to 280lbs.
    My horns grow throughout all of my life.
    There is only 2,500 of us left in the Mexico and 780 in the United States.
    Due to overhunting and habitat loss it is hard to find water resources nowadays.

    Desert

    Overhunting and habitat loss are the leading causes of endangered species in desert ecosystems.

    Polar Bears

    I am a Polar Bear! I can run up to 24mph!
    My species can weight up to 2,866 lbs for males and for females they can weigh up to 1,543 lbs.
    That is like having a grand piano and a telephone pole fall on top of you.
    My diet consists of seals, walrus, narwhals, belugas, and carcasses of animals.
    Did you know I have black skin under my thick white fur, a blue tongue, and three eyelids?
    Impressive huh?
    Unfortunately, due to global warming I am losing my habitat.
    Currently, there is only 25,000 polar bears left in the world.

    Narwal

    I am a Narwal.
    My diet consists of green land halibut, arctic/polar cod, squid, and shrimp.
    Did you know that my tusks can grow up to 10 feet long?
    I have 4 different color stages.
    When my species is at newborn stage they are blue/gray.
    Juveniles are blue/black and adults are mottled gray.
    When we are older we turn white.
    Due to oil and gas development we have been losing our habitat.
    Making my species easier targets for polar bears and killer whales!
    Our population is 75,000 we are near to be threatened by the IUCN.

    Wood Bisons

    I am a Wood Bison! I live anywhere that has a forest or mountains.
    I can weigh up to 2,000 pounds!
    I am about as heavy as a whales' testicles!
    My diet consists of grasses, sedges, forbes, silverberries, and willow leaves.
    I am an excellent swimmer!
    Due to humans over hunting my species, changes in my habitat, and severe cold winters.
    My species is almost extinct! We are under the ESA or Endangered Species Act.
    The only place to see my species is in Canada.
    There is only 300 of us left in the entire world.

    Mountain Caribou

    I am a Mountain Caribou.
    I live in Western Alberta and British Columbia.
    My diet contains lichens, mosses, grasses, ferns, and leaves of deciduous.
    I can weigh up to 385 pounds. That's the weight of a pool table!
    I can run up to 31mph! Don't make us mad because you can't outrun us!
    My species population is 10,000.
    Due to loss of our habitat from logging.
    That makes my species easy targets for other predators!

    Tundra

    Global warming and human-mining activity are the leading causes of endangered species in tundra ecosystems.

    Blue Whale

    I am a Blue Whale.
    I am the largest and loudest animal on the planet!
    I can weigh up to 330,000 pounds.
    I spend summers in the polar regions and migrate to equatorial regions in winter.
    Krill is the only food I can eat.
    Due to pollution, fishing, boat strikes, and climate change there is a loss of krill.
    There is only 25,000 left of my species.

    Vaquita

    I am a Vaquita.
    I am porpoise with a dark coloration around the eyes and mouth.
    I am 5 feet and 120 pounds.
    I am the world's rarest marine mammal.
    We are only found close to the shore of the Northern Gulf of California.
    Due to illegal fishing/nets our population is decreasing.
    Can you imagine there is only 10 of my species left in the world??????

    Florida Manatee

    I am a Florida Manatee.
    I can weigh up to 1,000 pounds and grow up to 10 feet.
    My diet consists of seagrass, algae, and vegetation.
    I can be found in the Coastal and Inland waters of the Southeastern US.
    Due to climate change, vessel collision, and pollution.
    Caused my species to lose our habitat, warm waters, and seagrass.
    Which has made my species become very stressed and starve to death.

    Whale Sharks

    I am a Whale Shark.
    I am the largest shark and one of the largest fishes alive!
    I can weigh up to 11 tons and grow to 40 feet big.
    I can be found in all tropical oceans.
    Yet, due to humans poaching my species for my meat, fins, and oil in the international market.
    Along with pollution and tourism that are being a threat to my species.
    Our population is slowly decreasing. There is 238,000 left of us.

    Marine

    Pollution, overfishing and climate change are the leading causes of endangered species in marine ecosystems.
