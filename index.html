    • Our Site's Mission

    Endangered Species & Ecosystems is an interactable site where you can explore different ecosystems and learn about a few endangered species that live within those biomes. These species can range from conversation status of vulnerable to nearly extinct.

    Jaguar

    “I am a Jaguar in the large cat species. I can be up to 6 ft and one inch.
    I am the third largest cat in the world. I am pale yellow to tan colored fur with spots.
    I have a very powerful bite; I can break through a turtle's shell. But my habitat is in DANGER!
    Due to fragmentation meaning big parts of my habitat are destroyed that it is hard to regrow over time!
    long with human poaching who wants my fur! There's only 15,000 jaguars left in the world!
    If I go extinct the food chain will be disrupted and the rodent population will rise!”

    Tropical

    Deforestation is the leading cause of endangered species in tropical and jungle ecosystems.

    Desert

    Overhunting and habitat loss are the leading causes of endangered species in desert ecosystems.

    Tundra

    Global warming and human-mining activity are the leading causes of endangered species in tundra ecosystems.

    Marine

    Pollution, overfishing and climate change are the leading causes of endangered species in marine ecosystems.
